Healthcare marketing in India sits between two pressures. On one side, patients are choosing clinics with the same research instinct they'd use to buy a phone — reviews, photos, prices, proximity. On the other, the category is trust-heavy, compliance-sensitive, and regulated in ways most agencies don't respect.

We work with dental practices, aesthetic clinics, diagnostic centres, and multi-speciality clinics to build sites that convert that research into booked appointments — without the grey-area tactics that get ad accounts banned.

## Pain points we see in healthcare

- **Trust barrier.** Patients are cautious. A slow site, a broken image, or a stale phone number and they're gone.
- **Booking friction.** If booking an appointment takes more than 3 clicks, most users drop.
- **Review dependency.** 70%+ of local healthcare decisions are influenced by Google reviews. Yet most clinic sites surface them poorly, or not at all.
- **Compliance risk.** Before/after photos, outcome claims, and pricing language have to follow DCI, MCI, and platform policies. Bad agencies get accounts permanently banned.

## What we build for healthcare clinics

- **Booking-first design.** Appointment CTA in nav, in every hero, on every service page, at the end of every scroll.
- **Online booking integrations** — Practo, Calendly, Hubspot Meetings, or a native form that posts to your practice management system
- **Service-specific landing pages** — one page for "root canal Andheri," one for "Invisalign Powai," one for "full-mouth rehabilitation Mumbai." Long-tail keywords are the whole game.
- **Review-led social proof** — Google reviews rendered natively on the site, not screenshots
- **Location-specific schema** — `MedicalBusiness`, `Physician`, `Review`, hours, speciality — so your Google Business Profile and site reinforce each other
- **Review-led ad creative** — patient testimonials (with consent), before/after (where compliant), and calm explainer reels
- **Meta + Google ads targeted to a 3–8km catchment** — tighter radius, higher intent, lower CPL

> Healthcare creative has to be calm, clear, and compliant. "Shock" creative gets engagement but banned accounts. We optimise for the approval rate, not the first-week vanity metric.

## FAQ

**Can I run paid ads for medical procedures on Meta and Google?**

Yes, with care. Certain categories (cosmetic surgery, weight-loss, fertility) have tighter platform rules; others (dental, dermatology, general diagnostics) are more permissive. We review every creative for compliance before launch. We've run healthcare accounts for years without a single permanent ban.

**How important are Google reviews?**

Enormously. We'd rank it second only to having a proper website. For most clinic clients we'd suggest a dedicated review-generation flow (polite post-visit SMS or WhatsApp) before spending the first rupee on ads. A clinic with 20 reviews competing against one with 300 will lose to it every time, regardless of ad budget.

**What's a realistic CPL for dental or aesthetic clinics in Indian metros?**

Dental: ₹250–₹700 per enquiry. Aesthetic/cosmetic: ₹500–₹1,500. Diagnostic: ₹150–₹500. These depend heavily on city, treatment, and creative quality — treat them as starting bands, not guarantees.

**Can you work with our existing practice management software?**

Usually yes. We've integrated with Practo, CareStack, and several custom systems. If a deep integration isn't possible, we'll at minimum get leads into your inbox or WhatsApp with enough context for your front desk to convert them.
